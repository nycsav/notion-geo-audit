Fix what the audit finds.

Four generators that produce the exact files the audit checks for. Fill the fields, copy the output, put it on your site. Everything runs in your browser — nothing is sent anywhere.

🤖 robots.txt AI-allowlist builder

Each AI engine reads your site with its own named crawler. Naming them with Allow: / is an explicit welcome signal — and it survives any future tightening of your "*" rules.
Where it goes: the file must live at yoursite.org/robots.txt. On a *.notion.site you can't edit it — connect a custom domain first.

📜 llms.txt generator

An emerging standard: a short plain-text map that AI assistants read first — who you are, and your best pages. Think of it as a menu you hand the engine instead of letting it wander.
Where it goes: yoursite.org/llms.txt — a plain text file at your site root.

🪪 Identity schema (JSON-LD)

This is how AI engines disambiguate you from similarly-named entities. Without it, an engine answering "what is yoursite.org?" is guessing — and sometimes it guesses a different company.
Where it goes: paste the whole block into your home page's <head>. On Notion or no-code builders, use the custom-code / site-settings injection if available.

❓ FAQ schema generator

FAQs are the most directly quotable format for AI answers — a real question, answered in plain language, marked up so machines know it's a Q&A.
Tip: use 3–6 questions people actually ask you. Put the same Q&As visibly on the page — schema should describe real content, not replace it.
